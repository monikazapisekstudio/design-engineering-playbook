## Primary academic source

**Kano, N. (1984).** *Attractive Quality and Must-be Quality.* Hinshitsu (Quality)
/ *Journal of the Japanese Society for Quality Control*, 14(2), 39–44.

The Kano Model — the four (or five) category classification of product
attributes by their relationship to customer satisfaction — was introduced in
this paper. The category labels, the 2-question (functional / dysfunctional)
survey methodology, and the satisfaction curve are all defined in this work.
The model itself (the idea, the categories, the questionnaire) is a published
academic framework and is not copyrightable; only specific *expressions* of it
are. The wording in this skill is original.

## Practitioner sources

**Spool, J.** *Building a Winning UX Strategy Using the Kano Model* (talk /
article, UIE / Centers of Excellence).

The quote *"Innovation is not about adding new inventions, it is about adding
new value"* in `SKILL.md` and the framing of "Experience Rot" are attributed
to Jared Spool's UX strategy work. The Spool talk synthesizes the academic
Kano model for product/UX practice.

The phrasing in this skill — "Start with NO", the "adopted child" metaphor, the
specific rot-signal list — is original synthesis, not lifted from any Spool
publication.
